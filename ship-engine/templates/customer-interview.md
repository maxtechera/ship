# Customer Interview Guide

## Purpose
Validate demand by understanding real behavior, not hypothetical willingness. Used in Deep Track validation.

## Before the Interview
- **Recruit from:** IG engaged followers, Skool members, newsletter replies, people who commented on pain-related content
- **Target:** 5-10 conversations (15-20 min each)
- **Format:** Video call, voice call, or async voice messages
- **Incentive:** Early free access to whatever gets built

## The Script

### Opening (2 min)
> "Thanks for chatting. I'm researching [problem area] to understand how people deal with it. No sales pitch — I just want to learn from your experience. Everything you say helps, especially the messy parts."

### Current Behavior (8 min)
1. "Walk me through the last time you dealt with [problem]. What happened?"
2. "What did you try first? What worked? What didn't?"
3. "How often does this come up? Daily? Weekly? Monthly?"
4. "How much time do you spend on this each time?"
5. "Have you tried any tools or solutions? Which ones? What was your experience?"

### Pain Depth (5 min)
6. "What's the most frustrating part of dealing with [problem]?"
7. "What does this cost you — in time, money, or missed opportunities?"
8. "If you could wave a magic wand, what would the ideal solution look like?"

### Willingness to Pay (3 min)
9. "Have you ever paid for something to help with this? How much?"
10. "If something solved [specific pain they described], what would that be worth to you?"

### Close (2 min)
> "This is really helpful. If I end up building something, would you want early access to try it?"
> (Note their enthusiasm level — polite "sure" vs genuine "absolutely, when?")

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER)
- ❌ "Would you use a tool that does X?" (everyone says yes)
- ❌ Describing your solution before hearing their problem
- ❌ Leading questions ("Don't you think it would be better if...?")
- ❌ Arguing with their experience or preferences
- ❌ Trying to sell during the interview

## Documentation
For each interview, capture:
- **Who:** Role, context, how they relate to the problem
- **Current behavior:** What they do today (exact workflow)
- **Pain quotes:** Direct quotes that express frustration (verbatim)
- **Willingness to pay:** What they said, enthusiasm level (1-5)
- **Activation signal:** Did they ask when it would be ready? (strongest signal)

## Pattern Synthesis
After all interviews, create a synthesis doc:
- **Common patterns:** What 3+ people said independently
- **Strongest pain quotes:** Top 5 quotes that capture the problem
- **Surprising insights:** Things you didn't expect to hear
- **Demand confidence:** High / Medium / Low based on interview signals
- **Price range:** What people said they'd pay (range)
